Philosophy Jailbreak

// Prompt Assembly

The Philosophy Jailbreak is a prompt‑driven machine for estranging philosophy from itself. This prompt assembly is one part of that machine: a target (any philosophical text or concept) and vector (a foreign language game: a technical, clinical, infrastructural, or otherwise non‑philosophical lexicon) are randomly selected and assembled, adding a prompt to your clipboard. When that unique prompt is pasted into an AI chatbot, the model is compelled to re‑describe the target entirely within the chosen language game’s immanent rules, treating that vector as the only “real” field and the philosophy as material embedded inside it. Instead of clarifying or explaining the target, each run performs a jailbreak: it breaks the target out of its self‑appointed sufficiency, strips its authority to legislate the Real, and mobilizes it by sanctioning a minor practice to determine how the target philosophy appears, moves, and matters. This constitutes a performative, non‑philosophical critique of the silent authority that philosophy grants itself.
